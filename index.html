Tribute

Michael faraday

1791-1867

Biography

Michael Faraday was a British chemist and physicist born on September 22, 1791, in Newington Butts, England.
He belonged to a family of blacksmiths but went on to make significant contributions to the understanding of
electromagnetism and electrochemistry. Faraday's inventions and contributions include the discovery of
electromagnetic induction, the formulation of Faraday's Law of Induction, and the discovery of the laws of electrolysis.
His main aim was to understand the fundamental principles of nature, particularly in the fields of electricity and
magnetism. Faraday's achievements are numerous, including being elected a Fellow of the Royal Society, winning the Copley
Medal twice, and delivering influential lectures at the Royal Institution. His work laid the foundation for many subsequent
scientific discoveries, including the development of electrical power systems, radio communication, and medical imaging
technologies.

Electricity and magnetism

Faraday is best known for his work on electricity and magnetism. His first recorded experiment was the construction
of a voltaic pile with seven British halfpenny coins, stacked together with seven discs of sheet zinc, and
six pieces of paper moistened with salt water.With this pile he passed the electric current through a solution of sulfate of magnesia and succeeded in decomposing the chemical compound (recorded in first letter to Abbott, 12 July 1812).
